Intro

My CV will tell you that I’m a Telecommunications & Information Engineer and PhD candidate at Tampere University specializing in image compression. It will also say I worked for 3 years as a Research Scientist in X-ray imaging, where I turned company and customer needs into scalable solutions based on image processing. By the way, check out my awesome work.

As a matter of fact I am a people-centric technologist with deep roots in multimedia. My sweet spot is computational imaging and computer vision. I love collaborating across R&D, product, and support to solve hard problems, explaining the “why,” unblocking the “how,” and ensuring solutions stick. Inside and outside the office, I’m the “let’s loop everyone in” type: I speak at meetups, run and organize workshops, and enjoy supporting teams and customers. When I’m not building imaging pipelines or endlessly talking with someone, you’ll find me digging into IT and gaming, or recharging with electronic music, soccer, and the latest sci-fi/fantasy series.

Work

Let's not get bogged down in official titles, shall we? My career path is less a straight line and more a series of very interesting detours. As you may have read in the intro I am, on paper, a Telecommunications & Information Engineer and a PhD candidate at Tampere University, which is just a long-winded way of saying I'm "technically" good at making pictures take up less space. On this path of becoming an expert in digital squishiness, I spent three years as a Research Scientist in X-ray imaging, basically moonlighting as a detective for the invisible. I also dabbled in image processing as a research assistant at the Finnish Hub for Development and Validation of Integrated Approaches (let's just call it FHAIVE) for short.

And my Italian chapter? That was a wild ride. I've been everything from an RF designer to a Network Engineer, a Technical Instructor and even a NOC operator. You know, the kind of person who knows exactly what to do when the internet goes out at 3 AM.

    • About

    I'm a 33-year-old who, for the better part of three decades, was a full-time Roman; navigating ancient cobblestones, missing delayed buses and surviving a chaotic life with a certain flair was my go-to. About 10 years ago, I decided to swap the Colosseum for the cold, and I've been successfully faking it as a Finn ever since. My life is a delightful mess of cultural confusion, where I'm constantly trying to remember if I'm supposed to be sipping espresso or enduring a sauna session. The Roman in me still dreams of summer heat or chaotic football games at the stadium, but the new, faux-Finnish me has learned to appreciate the subtle art of not complaining about the weather. So, if you're looking for someone who brings a little bit of ancient history and a lot of dry humor to the table, you've got the right guy. Just don't ask me for directions to the nearest pizzeria—I'm still figuring out how to survive on herring and rye bread.
